What Is an ATS Resume and Why Does It Matter?

An ATS resume is a resume formatted so Applicant Tracking Systems can read your contact details, sections, skills, and work history correctly.

That matters because 75% of resumes are filtered out by ATS before a human sees them, 99% of Fortune 500 companies use Applicant Tracking Systems, and "Resumes optimized for ATS are 40% more likely to get interviews." Your resume still needs real experience and honest achievements, but clean formatting gives that experience a fair chance to be seen.

Tooliest keeps the resume structure simple: standard headings, single-column layout, real text, and export formats that recruiters can open. The goal is not to beat ATS systems with tricks. The goal is to make your best evidence easy for both software and humans to understand.

ATS Resume Score Checker: How the Score Is Calculated

The ATS score estimates how complete, readable, and keyword-ready your resume is before you apply.

Tooliest calculates a free local score across six dimensions: contact information, section completeness, achievement quality, keyword density, resume length, and profile completeness. A score above 75 is considered strong because it usually means the resume has the core sections, enough substance, action-oriented bullets, and relevant keywords. For deeper targeting, the AI enrichment compares the generated resume to a job description and gives role-specific keyword feedback.

What is a good ATS resume score?

A score from 75 to 89 is strong, and 90 or higher is ATS ready. Lower scores usually mean the resume needs more detail, clearer sections, stronger bullets, or better keyword alignment.

How to Make a Resume Pass ATS Screening

You improve ATS performance by making the resume easy to parse and closely aligned with the role.

  1. Enter personal information and a focused summary - Add your name, email, phone, location, LinkedIn, target role, and a 2-3 sentence summary.
  2. Add work experience with achievement bullet points - Use action verbs and real results instead of responsibility-only descriptions.
  3. Add education, skills, projects, and certifications - Include relevant keywords only when you can support them.
  4. Use AI to improve sections - Rewrite the summary, enhance bullets, and optimize skills without changing unrelated fields.
  5. Check your real-time ATS score - Fix weak dimensions before you generate or export the resume.
  6. Choose a resume template and download - Use Classic, Modern, or Compact, then export PDF or DOCX.
  7. Generate a matching cover letter - Use the Cover Letter tab when the application benefits from a tailored note.

For keyword review outside the resume workflow, the keyword density checker can help you compare repeated terms without stuffing your resume.

Quick Facts About ATS Resume Optimization

What percentage of Fortune 500 companies use ATS?
99% of Fortune 500 companies use Applicant Tracking Systems to screen, organize, or route resumes before recruiter review.
What ATS score should I aim for?
A score above 75 is strong for most applications. A score above 90 usually means the resume is complete, keyword-aware, and easy to parse.
What is the ideal resume length for ATS systems?
Most ATS-optimized resumes should be 350-700 words. Under 200 words is usually sparse, while more than 900 words may be too long for many roles.
How many keywords should a resume include?
Use enough role-specific keywords to reflect your real experience. Keyword density should come from honest skills, tools, job titles, and achievements.
Which resume format is safest for ATS?
A single-column PDF or DOCX with standard headings, real text, and no tables or sidebars is the safest format for most ATS systems.
Do action verbs help ATS matching?
Action verbs help recruiters and scoring tools understand impact. Start bullets with verbs like Led, Built, Improved, Reduced, Automated, or Delivered.
Should every achievement have a number?
Use numbers when they are true and defensible. Metrics such as percentages, revenue, time saved, volume, accuracy, or cost reduction make impact clearer.
Do cover letters still matter?
Yes. 83% of hiring managers read cover letters, and 94% say they influence interview decisions, especially when the letter is specific to the company.
