How Do I Create an HTML Email Signature Without Signing Up?

Create a professional HTML email signature in 60 seconds, preview it live, and copy table-based HTML for Gmail, Outlook, or Apple Mail without signing up.

Why use a browser-based email signature generator?
Most signature builders hide the export behind CRM signups, paid plans, or hosted image requirements. Tooliest keeps the whole workflow in one tab: fill in your details, preview the result instantly, and copy the HTML you can paste straight into Gmail, Outlook, or Apple Mail.

Why table-based HTML?
Email clients are far stricter than websites. Gmail strips head styles, Outlook ignores a lot of modern CSS, and Apple Mail has its own quirks. This generator uses table layouts, inline styles, and email-safe fonts so the copied output is much more reliable across clients.

How to Add an HTML Email Signature in Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail

After you generate the signature, copy the HTML and paste it into your email client's signature editor. Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail all support pasted HTML signatures, but they behave more reliably when the markup is table-based and fully inline styled.

What Should a Professional Email Signature Include?

A professional signature usually includes your name, role, company, direct contact details, and one or two brand cues like a profile photo, company logo, or accent color. The goal is to make it easy to contact you without turning the signature into a miniature landing page.

Why Table-Based HTML Matters for Email Signatures

Website layouts can rely on flexbox and modern CSS, but email clients still behave like a much older web. Table-based HTML with inline styles gives Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, and mobile email apps a better chance of rendering the signature consistently.

Can I Make a Professional Email Signature Online for Free?

Yes. A browser-based email signature generator lets you fill in your contact details, choose a template, upload a photo or logo, and copy the final HTML without creating an account. That is ideal when you need something polished quickly and just want to paste it into your email settings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add an HTML signature to Gmail?

In Gmail, open Settings, choose See all settings, scroll to the Signature section, create a new signature, click the source code icon in the editor, paste the generated HTML, and save your settings.

Will this signature work in Outlook?

Yes. Tooliest creates table-based HTML with inline styles, which is the structure Outlook expects. The Copy for Outlook option adds extra Outlook-friendly wrapping for safer pasting.

Can I add my photo to my email signature?

Yes. Upload a PNG or JPG photo and Tooliest embeds it directly into the generated HTML. If you skip the photo, the signature falls back to an initials avatar.

Is this email signature generator really free?

Completely free, forever. There is no account, no paid unlock, and no watermark added to the signature.

What is the best font for an email signature?

Stick to email-safe fonts such as Arial, Georgia, Verdana, Trebuchet MS, and Tahoma. Those render more consistently than web fonts inside Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail.
